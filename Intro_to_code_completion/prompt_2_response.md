- Why do we hiccup?
  - Hiccups are involuntary contractions of the diaphragm followed by sudden closing of the vocal cords. They can be triggered by irritation, eating too quickly, cold drinks, excitement, or stretching the stomach.

- What’s the difference between a comet and an asteroid?
  - Comets are icy bodies from the outer solar system that develop glowing comas and tails when near the Sun. Asteroids are rocky or metallic bodies mostly in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter.

- Why does popcorn pop when heated?
  - Each kernel has water inside. When heated, the water turns to steam, pressure builds until the kernel’s shell breaks, and the starch expands into the fluffy popcorn.

- How do airplanes stay in the air?
  - Lift generated by wings (air moving faster over the top surfaces creates lower pressure). Engine thrust overcomes drag, and stability comes from the tail and wing design. Bernoulli’s principle and Newton’s third law both help explain it.

- Who invented the first mechanical clock?
  - Early mechanical clocks appeared in medieval Europe (around the 13th century). Clockmakers in places like Italy and the Holy Roman Empire contributed to their development; there isn’t a single inventor.

- What causes a rainbow?
  - Light from the Sun enters raindrops, is refracted, reflected inside the drop, and refracted again as it exits. This disperses light into a spectrum, producing a rainbow.

- How does a piano produce sound?
  - When keys are pressed, a felt-covered hammer strikes strings, causing them to vibrate. The soundboard and other parts amplify these vibrations, producing musical tones.

- Why is the sky blue during the day but red at sunset?
  - Rayleigh scattering: shorter blue wavelengths scatter in all directions by air molecules, making the sky look blue. At sunset, the Sun’s light passes through more atmosphere; longer red wavelengths dominate as blue light is scattered out of the line of sight.

- What is the purpose of fingerprints?

- How do penguins keep warm in Antarctica?

- Why do cats purr?
  - Purring is produced by the rhythmic movement of the laryngeal muscles and the flow of air. It’s associated with contentment, self-soothing, and sometimes healing, though cats may purr when in pain or stressed too.

- How do GPS systems determine your location?

- How do plants convert sunlight into energy?
